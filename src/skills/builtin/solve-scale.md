# Solve Scale Business Framework

> Get to revenue fast. Don't get lost in the vibe of building.
> Designed by Salik Shah. Licensed CC BY-SA 4.0.

---

## The Two Stages

```
SOLVE  →  identify & solve a real customer problem
SCALE  →  establish brand, systems, and strategy for exponential growth
```

Start at SOLVE. Don't skip to SCALE until you have early traction.

---

## SOLVE Stage

### 1. Purpose
*Why does your company exist beyond making a profit?*

- What is your driving purpose, reason, or belief?
- How does it align with what you want to do?
- What broader purpose does your business serve?

**Examples:** OpenAI: "AGI benefits all humanity." Slack: "Make work simpler." Amazon: "Most customer-centric company."

---

### 2. Problem
*What real problem are you solving for real people?*

- What are the main problems your target customers face?
- How do these problems affect their daily lives or work?
- What existing solutions are available — and why do they fall short?

---

### 3. Solution
*How does your product solve it — better than anything else?*

- How does your product/service solve the identified problem?
- What makes your solution unique or better than existing alternatives?
- How will it evolve as technology or market conditions change?

---

### 4. Value Proposition
*Why should a customer choose you over everyone else?*

- What value do you deliver to customers?
- How do you differ from competitors?
- One sentence: "We help [customer] do [X] so they can [outcome]."

---

### 5. MVP
*What is the smallest version that proves the idea?*

- What is the simplest form of your product that solves the problem?
- What key features does it include — and what does it deliberately exclude?
- How will you know if the MVP is successful? (one measurable signal)

---

### 6. Metrics
*What does success look like in 30 / 60 / 90 days?*

- What are the key metrics that indicate success?
- How will you measure them?
- What SMART targets have you set for the next 3–6 months?

---

### 7. Customer Segments
*Who exactly are you building for?*

- Who are your target customers?
- What defines them (demographic, psychographic, behavioral)?
- How does your product meet their specific needs?

---

### 8. Channels
*How do you reach and serve your customers?*

- What channels will you use to reach them?
- How will you communicate your value proposition through these channels?
- How will you measure channel effectiveness?

---

### 9. Cost Structure
*What does it cost to operate and deliver value?*

- What are the significant costs in producing your product?
- How do costs compare to your pricing and revenue model?
- What will you do to control costs as you scale?

---

### 10. Revenue Model
*How do you make money — and why will customers pay?*

- How will you generate revenue?
- What are the main revenue sources?
- How does your pricing fit with customers' willingness to pay?

---

## SCALE Stage

*(Reach this stage after validated traction from SOLVE.)*

### Brand: Mission, Vision, Culture
- Mission statement (what you do)
- Vision (future you're building toward)
- Culture values (how your team works)

### Roles: Leadership, Teams, Partnerships
- Key leaders and their roles
- Team structure aligned to goals
- Strategic partnerships

### Systems
- Key systems supporting operations
- How systems scale with growth
- Technology and process backbone

### Strategy
- Overall competitive strategy
- How you differentiate from competitors
- 3–5 year growth plan

### Growth Marketing
- Customer acquisition strategies
- Retention and expansion plays
- Metrics for marketing effectiveness

### Venture Capital (when ready)
- Why raise VC? What problem does capital solve?
- How funds will be deployed
- Return on investment thesis

### M&A (when ready)
- Strategic fit criteria
- Integration approach
- Examples: acquire for tech, talent, or market access

### Continuous Innovation
- Culture of innovation: how you foster it
- Processes and systems for innovation
- Recent product innovations and learnings
